Joint Inspection of Chemistry Date: 01/01/2024
Purpose:
To check and report the chemistry of the cast & rolled products jointly with Contract
Manufacturer team
Scope:
Final liquid metal chemistry at Ladle or Cast billet
Rolled product
Applicable standard:
IS 1786 2008 & Amendment 3(2017) for specification and sampling requirements
Teams and Responsibilities:
Sl Team Responsibility
No

2 SGS QC Engineers e Verification of the test method, cross checking of the
test results and clearance of the chemistry as per IS

e Preparation of Joint Inspection Report
3 JSW One Operation Ensure the chemistry as per JSW One product standard
Manager and IS 1786 requirements for final clearance and approval
e Pre-testing Verifications:
1. Status of Calibration certificate issued by NABL accredited agency for the optical
spectrometer in use to be verified by SGS QC Engineers before joint inspection.
If the instrument is out of calibration or calibrated by non-NABL agency, the
concerned spectrometer shall not be used for chemical testing of JSW One
| heats.

2. SGS QC Engineer shall check whether Certified Reference Material (CRM)
samples accompanied with test certificate issued by NABL accredited laboratory
is available.

lf the CRM sample is not certified by NABL accredited laboratory, test values

shall not be accepted.

3. SGS Engineer shall check whether the chemistry of the master samples
approximately matches with working range of grades under production.

If CRM sample chemistry does not match with working range of grade chemistry,

Contract Manufacturer must be intimated to procure the correct CRM sample

from NABL accredited source.

e Sampling plan:
€ 1. Minimum one final ladle/cast billet sample per heat.
2. Minimum 2 nos. product sample per rolling lot
e Joint Testing Verification:

1. Contract Manufacturer shall calibrate the machine with CRM sample at the
beginning of the first shift, after each restart of the spectrometer and every
change in the grade.

2. Record of the calibration shall be verified by the SGS QC Engineer on daily
basis. SGS QC Engineer shall verify that each element is within the range
specified in the CRM.
lf any of the element is out of range, re-calibration to be carried out or a bias
to be considered for this element after at least three repeat tests on CRM.

3. SGS QC Engineers shall verify in the record that channels of all essential
elements (C, Mn, S, P, Cr, Cu, Ni, Mo & N2) are functional

4. SGS Engineer shall witness the testing process and perform the joint
inspection with Contract Manufacturer at the time of steel making/casting.
5. The final ladle/cast billet chemistry report to be shared by Contract
Manufacturer and SGS QC Engineer to all concerned JSW One Quality and
Operation teams.
The heat shall be accepted for rolling of SSW One materials only if all
essential elements are within IS 1786 standard.
6. In case of any deviation from JSW One product standard that specified
narrower ranges and additional requirements, SGS QC Engineer to
| immediately inform JSW One operation manager for decision on acceptance.
As and when required, JSW One operation manager can consult with
Regional Quality Manager and Quality Head for decision making.
7. If joint inspection is not possible by SGS QC Engineer at the time of steel
making process, the cast billet sample of every heat to be preserved by
