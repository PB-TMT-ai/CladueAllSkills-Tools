## 20-Slide Specifications

### ACT I: THE OPPORTUNITY (Situation)

**Slide 1 — Title**
- Title: "India GCC Landscape: Strategic Analysis for Financial Services"
- Description: C-suite decision document covering location strategy, operating model, and talent playbook for BFSI GCCs
- So What: Board-ready strategy, not a market overview

**Slide 2 — India GCC Market: The Inflection Point**
- Title: "The India GCC Market Has Reached an Inflection Point"
- Description: 1800+ GCCs generating $65B revenue with BFSI at 35% share and 230+ centers. Projected 2400+ GCCs and $100B by 2030. DORA enforcement and 10.4% salary inflation make 2025-2026 the decision window
- Key data: 1,800+ GCCs, $65B, 230+ BFSI, $40.43B BFSI market, 12.54% CAGR, 63% CXO innovation centrality
- So What: BFSI is the largest GCC vertical growing 3x the market — this is table stakes, not optional

### ACT II: LOCATION ANALYSIS (Complication)

**Slide 3 — BFSI-Weighted Location Scorecard**
- Title: "BFSI-Weighted Location Scorecard Across Four Hubs"
- Description: Four cities scored with BFSI-specific weights — Talent 20%, Ecosystem 15%, Cost 15%, Retention 15%, Regulatory 10%, Infra 10%, Connectivity 8%, QoL 7%. Bangalore leads at 8.1/10 followed by Hyderabad 7.2, Delhi NCR 6.8, Pune 6.5
- So What: Equal-weighted scorecards mask BFSI priorities — when you weight for financial services, Bangalore leads but Hyderabad is closer than expected

**Slide 4 — Bangalore: Established Leader with a Cost Ceiling**
- Title: "Bangalore: The Established Leader Facing Saturation Risk"
- Description: 870+ GCCs with 42% share and 32% of FinCrime talent. Home to Goldman Sachs, JP Morgan, Wells Fargo. GCC Policy 2024-2029 targeting 500 new GCCs. Challenge — highest cost index 100 and 16-20% attrition with all major banks competing for the same 1M professionals
- So What: 42% share means saturation — Goldman, JPM, Citi, Wells Fargo all fight over the same talent pool

**Slide 5 — Hyderabad: Fastest-Growing Challenger**
- Title: "Hyderabad: 85% of Bangalore's Capability at 78% of the Cost"
- Description: 355+ GCCs with 20% banking share and fastest new additions. Rents 20-30% below Bangalore. Vanguard scaling from 300 to 2300 by 2029. TS-iPASS 15-day single window clearance. HITEC City and Financial District are world-class infrastructure
- So What: Hyderabad is the strongest alternative primary hub — the cost-capability gap with Bangalore is closing fast

**Slide 6 — Pune: Best Retention, Limited Scale**
- Title: "Pune: Where Retention Saves $3-5M Annually per 1000 FTE"
- Description: 250+ GCCs with best retention at 12-14% attrition, 5-8 points below Bangalore. Cost index 75-80 with 84% graduate employability. Barclays operates 9000 people here, its largest outside London. Challenge — no international flights and only 6% of FinCrime talent
- So What: 6-8 points lower attrition saves $3-5M annually per 1000 FTE — deploy for stable long-tenure operations

**Slide 7 — Delhi NCR: Connectivity and Regulatory Powerhouse**
- Title: "Delhi NCR: Direct HQ Flights and Regulatory DNA"
- Description: 300+ GCCs across Gurugram, Noida, Delhi. Best airport with 79.2M passengers and direct US/UK/EU flights. Home to American Express, Deutsche Bank, HSBC. Proximity to RBI, SEBI provides regulatory talent advantage. UP GCC Policy offers 30-50% land subsidies
- So What: The only India hub where a London MD can fly direct to office — irreplaceable for front-office and regulatory functions

**Slide 8 — Strategic Location Recommendation**
- Title: "Hub-and-Spoke with BFSI Function-City Allocation"
- Description: Primary hub Bangalore or Hyderabad. Function mapping — Innovation and AI/ML to Bangalore, Operations and Scale to Hyderabad, Risk and Compliance to Pune, Client-facing and Regulatory to Delhi NCR. 70%+ of banking GCCs operate multiple centers. Tier-2 satellites offer 30-40% additional savings
- So What: The question is not which city — it is which functions go where. BFSI needs at least two hubs from Day 1

**Slide 9 — NEW: Financial Business Case**
- Title: "5-Year TCO Model: GCC Breaks Even at Month 24-30"
- Description: Three scenarios for 500 FTE — Pure outsourcing starts 30-70% cheaper but escalates 10-20% per renewal. Pure GCC breaks even Year 2-3 then delivers 15-20% savings. BOT hybrid achieves operational in 4-6 months with captive conversion by Month 18. Cost of inaction — 10-15% salary inflation per year of delay
- So What: A 500 FTE GCC delivers cumulative savings of 15-20% over outsourcing by Year 5 — the financial case is unambiguous at scale

### ACT III: OPERATING MODEL & TALENT (Resolution Part 1)

**Slide 10 — GCC vs Third-Party Comparison**
- Title: "GCC Wins on 6 of 8 Dimensions in a 3-Plus Year Horizon"
- Description: Comparison across Economics (upfront vs long-term cost), Control (IP ownership, innovation at 3.2x patents, compliance), and Risk (retention 40% higher, security, regulatory). Outsourcing only wins on speed and initial cost. GCC delivers 15-20% lower TCO post Year 3
- So What: GCC costs more upfront but wins decisively on control and risk — outsourcing only wins if you are planning to exit within 2 years

**Slide 11 — GCC Advantage: Innovation Engine**
- Title: "From Cost Center to Innovation Engine: The GCC Strategic Shift"
- Description: 15-20% lower TCO after Year 3 for a $50M annual spend that equals $7.5-10M savings. 3.2x more digital patents per $1M invested. 55% of enterprise tech products originate in GCCs. 80% of new GCCs prioritize AI/ML. Goldman Sachs grew from 300 support staff to 9000+ innovation professionals
- So What: GCCs are where the future products of financial services firms are being built — not back offices

**Slide 12 — Third-Party Risks: Regulatory and Financial**
- Title: "Escalating Third-Party Risks in a Post-DORA World"
- Description: 30% of data breaches from third parties in 2024. Average financial services breach costs $6.08M. Global financial penalties hit $4.6B in 2024, a 522% YoY increase. EU DORA Jan 2025 directly targets outsourcing concentration. RBI Master Direction requires board-level oversight. Outsourcing failure costs reach $0.5-1B per incident
- So What: Regulators are systematically closing the outsourcing window for critical financial services functions

**Slide 13 — NEW: Risk Register and Mitigation**
- Title: "Top 10 GCC Risks with Proven Mitigation Strategies"
- Description: Structured risk framework covering talent attrition (multi-city model plus ESOPs), regulatory change (GCC as in-house under RBI), data breach (captive perimeter), geopolitical disruption (non-aligned posture plus BCP), cost overrun (BOT model), knowledge transfer (90-day structured cycles), GCC-to-GCC poaching (differentiated EVP), real estate inflation (Tier-2 satellites)
- So What: Every risk on this register has a proven mitigation — inaction risk now exceeds GCC setup risk

**Slide 14 — Recommended Hybrid Operating Model**
- Title: "Hybrid GCC: Captive Core with BOT Entry and Selective Outsourcing"
- Description: GCC-owned core for risk, compliance, cybersecurity, innovation, AI/ML. Selective outsourcing for burst capacity and non-core BPO. BOT model for initial 4-6 month setup with captive conversion by Month 18. BOT adoption surged from under 10% to 40% of new GCCs. Mega GCC companies outsource 2x more proving hybrid works at scale
- So What: Start with BOT to derisk, transition to captive within 18 months, keep selective outsourcing for non-core

**Slide 15 — BFSI Talent Crisis**
- Title: "Why Financial Services GCCs Cannot Out-Hire Their Way to Growth"
- Description: 60% of GCC hiring from other GCCs creating circular competition. AI/ML supply meets only 49% of demand with 1M+ shortage by 2027. 75% of Gen Z intend to leave within 2 years. 9.9% salary increment 2025 rising to 10.4% in 2026. 15% attrition on 1000 FTE at $15-20K replacement cost equals $2.25-3M annually
- So What: At 10%+ salary inflation and 60% circular hiring, only a differentiated EVP breaks the cycle

**Slide 16 — BFSI-Specific EVP Framework**
- Title: "Five EVP Pillars Calibrated for Financial Services Talent"
- Description: BFSI-specific pillars — Global career paths with London/NYC/Singapore rotations. Regulatory mastery branding around DORA and Basel III expertise. Innovation at scale with $17B-budget labs not startup risk. Hybrid flexibility adopted by 95% of GCCs. Purpose through financial inclusion and ESG. Gartner Human Deal framework delivers 15% higher EVP satisfaction
- So What: A BFSI EVP must answer — will this GCC accelerate my career as fast as Goldman or JP Morgan would

**Slide 17 — Employer Branding Playbook**
- Title: "Only 25-30% of GCCs Invest in Branding: The First-Mover Arbitrage"
- Description: Three-phase roadmap — Phase 1 Months 1-3 fix Glassdoor and launch employee advocacy with 8x engagement. Phase 2 Months 4-9 campus partnerships with IITs and LinkedIn thought leadership. Phase 3 Months 10-18 Great Place to Work certification and awards. ROI — 43% lower cost-per-hire, 28% lower turnover, 50% more qualified applicants
- So What: Only 25-30% invest in branding — this is the single largest arbitrage opportunity in the talent war

### ACT IV: FROM ANALYSIS TO ACTION (Resolution Part 2)

**Slide 18 — NEW: 18-Month Implementation Roadmap**
- Title: "From Decision to Scale in 18 Months: Phased Implementation"
- Description: Phase 1 Months 0-4 — entity setup, city selection, BOT partner, leadership hires. Phase 2 Months 4-8 — first 50-100 FTE, knowledge transfer, EVP launch. Phase 3 Months 8-14 — scale to 200-300 FTE, second city, innovation lab. Phase 4 Months 14-18 — scale to 500 FTE, captive conversion, breakeven assessment

**Slide 19 — Five Strategic Recommendations**
- Title: "Five Specific Recommendations with Timelines and KPIs"
- Description: 1 Establish primary hub in Bangalore via BOT, 200 FTE Year 1. 2 Open Hyderabad secondary hub by Month 8 for scale. 3 Evaluate Pune spoke by Month 12 for retention-critical functions. 4 Launch differentiated EVP from Day 1 targeting 43% lower cost-per-hire. 5 Adopt hybrid model with under 30% outsourced FTE by Year 3. Cost of inaction — 10-15% salary inflation per year of delay
- So What: These five moves deliver a fully operational multi-city BFSI GCC within 18 months at 20%+ savings by Year 3
